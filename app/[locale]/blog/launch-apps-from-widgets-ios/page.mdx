# How to Launch Any App from a Home Screen or Lock Screen Widget on iPhone

**Quick answer:** iOS does not let you open a specific app from a stock widget with a single tap, but a dedicated app-launcher widget can. Apps like [FolderMini](/foldermini) turn a widget into a grid of real app icons — tap any tile and that app opens instantly, straight from your Home Screen or Lock Screen.

If you have ever wished you could tap a widget and jump straight into an app — instead of swiping through pages of icons or opening the App Library — this guide is for you.

---

## Why stock iOS widgets can't launch a specific app

Apple's built-in widgets are designed to *show information* (weather, calendar, battery) and, when tapped, open the app that owns the widget. They were never meant to work as a launcher for your other apps. That is why you cannot, out of the box, build a widget where each tile opens a different app.

The workaround is a **folder-widget app** — a small utility that renders a grid of app tiles inside a widget and opens the matching app when you tap a tile.

## Step-by-step: launch apps from a widget

1. **Install a folder-widget app.** Download an app-launcher widget such as [FolderMini](/foldermini) from the App Store. It's free.
2. **Build a folder.** Open the app and add the apps you want fast access to — group them by routine, project, or mood.
3. **Add the widget to your Home Screen.** Long-press an empty area of the Home Screen, tap the **+** in the top corner, search for the app, and choose a widget size (small, medium, or large).
4. **Or add it to your Lock Screen.** Long-press the Lock Screen, tap **Customize**, and add an accessory widget for your most-used apps.
5. **Tap to launch.** Every tile shows the real app icon. One tap opens that app directly.

## Common questions

**Can you open an app directly from a widget on iPhone?**
Yes — with an app-launcher widget like FolderMini, tapping a tile opens that app directly.

**Can I put an app launcher on the Lock Screen?**
Yes. iOS supports Lock Screen accessory widgets, and FolderMini lets you place your most-used apps there.

**Does this work without shortcuts or automations?**
Yes. A folder-widget app handles the launching natively, so you don't need to build anything in the Shortcuts app.
